# Literature Review Plan: Philosophical Perspectives on "Home"

## Research Objective

Create a focused, analytical literature review (3500-4000+ words) on philosophical perspectives on "home" to prepare for a 5000-word Encyclopedia article. Emphasis on analytical philosophy, applied ethics, and empirically-informed philosophical work.

## Temporal Scope

- **Primary**: 1990-present
- **Secondary**: Pre-1990 canonical works if frequently cited (e.g., Waldron's foundational homelessness work, early Young)

## Disciplinary Focus

1. **Primary**: Analytical philosophy, applied ethics, social/political philosophy
2. **Secondary**: Housing studies with philosophical framework, urban theory with ethical dimensions
3. **Minimal coverage**: Continental/phenomenological approaches (Heidegger, Bachelard, Levinas) - acknowledge but don't elaborate

## Domain Structure (7 Domains)

### Domain 1: Analytical Foundations - Home/House Distinctions
**Focus**: Conceptual analysis of "home," home vs. house, philosophical definitions
**Key questions**:
- How is "home" conceptually distinct from "house" or "dwelling"?
- What are the necessary and sufficient conditions for "home"?
- Analytical treatments of home as concept vs. physical space

**Priority authors**: David Benatar, analytical metaphysicians
**Priority journals**: Analysis, Philosophical Studies, Mind

### Domain 2: Homelessness Ethics
**Focus**: Ethical obligations regarding homelessness, rights of homeless persons
**Key questions**:
- What obligations do we have to the homeless?
- Rights to public space and sleeping rough (Waldron's thesis)
- Justice and homelessness

**Priority authors**: Jeremy Waldron (canonical), recent applied ethicists
**Priority journals**: Philosophy & Public Affairs, Ethics, Journal of Applied Philosophy

### Domain 3: Housing Justice & Distributive Justice
**Focus**: Housing rights, fair allocation, property theory applied to housing
**Key questions**:
- Is there a right to housing? On what grounds?
- How should housing be allocated justly?
- Property rights vs. housing access

**Priority authors**: Analytical political philosophers working on housing
**Priority journals**: Political Theory, Journal of Political Philosophy, Philosophy & Public Affairs

### Domain 4: Feminist Philosophical Perspectives
**Focus**: Gendered dimensions of home, domestic sphere, feminist analytical work
**Key questions**:
- How is home gendered? (analytical, not poststructuralist)
- Young's work on house and home
- Domestic labor, private/public distinction

**Priority authors**: Iris Marion Young (essential), other feminist analytical philosophers
**Priority journals**: Hypatia, Feminist Philosophy Quarterly, Ethics

### Domain 5: Philosophy of Place - Analytical Approaches
**Focus**: Home as meaningful place, attachment, rootedness (analytical treatments)
**Key questions**:
- What makes home a meaningful place?
- Place attachment and identity
- Philosophical geography (analytical, not phenomenological)

**Priority authors**: Jeff Malpas (selective - analytical work), others doing analytical philosophy of place
**Priority journals**: Philosophy & Geography, Environmental Ethics

### Domain 6: Migration, Displacement, and Exile
**Focus**: Philosophical work on displacement, migration ethics, exile
**Key questions**:
- What is lost in displacement?
- Rights to remain vs. rights to move
- Refugee ethics and home

**Priority authors**: Joseph Carens, migration ethicists with analytical rigor
**Priority journals**: Journal of Political Philosophy, Ethics & International Affairs

### Domain 7: NIMBY-ism, Urban Ethics, and Housing Policy
**Focus**: Applied ethics of housing policy, NIMBY-ism, urban justice
**Key questions**:
- Ethics of exclusionary housing policies
- Community resistance to housing developments
- Urban planning and justice

**Priority authors**: Margaret Kohn, urban political theorists with philosophical grounding
**Priority journals**: Journal of Applied Philosophy, Urban Studies (philosophical pieces)
