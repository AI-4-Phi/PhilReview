## VALIDATION METHODOLOGY

For each source, I conducted:
1. **WebSearch** for "[author name] [title] [year]"
2. **CrossRef/DOI verification** where DOIs provided
3. **Google Scholar confirmation** of author-title-year-venue match
4. **Criteria for FRAUDULENT**: Author(s) do not match, title mismatch, or paper does not exist

### Decision Criteria

- **VERIFIED**: ✓ Author, title, year, venue all match
- **FRAUDULENT**: ❌ Wrong authors (even if paper exists)
- **NOT VALIDATED**: ⚠️ Unable to verify (time constraints)

## FRAUD PATTERNS IDENTIFIED

### 1. Fake Author Substitution Pattern

**Modus Operandi**: Real papers exist with correct DOIs, but fraudulent BibTeX entries assign wrong authors

**Examples**:
- Real paper by Naik et al. → Falsely attributed to "Zhang et al."
- Real paper by Wen et al. → Falsely attributed to "Zhang et al."
- Real paper by Fragiadakis et al. → Falsely attributed to "Chi et al."
- Real paper by Bovo et al. → Falsely attributed to "Shneiderman"

### 2. Suspicious Author Name Pattern

Multiple fraudulent entries use generic or Chinese-sounding names:
- "Zhang, Lei"
- "Zhang, Min"
- "Zhang, Ge"
- "Chi, Na"
- "Kumar, Alok; Sharma, Sanjeev"

**Note**: Some Zhang authors ARE legitimate (e.g., zhang2025algorithmic is verified). The pattern is fake Zhang/generic names on high-value recent papers.

### 3. Temporal Pattern

**High-risk period**: 2024-2025 publications
- 9 of 16 fraudulent citations are from 2024-2025
- Recent preprints (arXiv) particularly vulnerable to fraud

### 4. Disciplinary Pattern

**Domain 4 (AI & Workplace) has 94% of fraud**:
- 15 of 16 fraudulent entries
- Likely because: rapidly evolving field, many preprints, high value for "latest" sources

### 5. Publication Type Pattern

**ArXiv preprints disproportionately fraudulent**:
- 6 of 16 fraudulent sources are arXiv preprints
- Preprints easier to fabricate authors for since less formal
